The Situation

For our 23 years since turnover, Longshore Lake employed an administrative staff of professionals dedicated solely to providing excellent service to its members.

But a recent series of unfortunate missteps by newly elected Directors compelled the entire office staff to quit, leaving us without ordinary services.

Faced with that, the Board of Directors hired a community management company, hoping to rescue the operation from rapid decline, an objective yet to be met. They have also shown little regard for the amenities that define Longshore Lake and placed at risk the enjoyment and property values of its members.

The introduction of a property management company adds complexity and cost to the administration of a community. It brings a new, for-profit, tier of management as well as its owners and investors, all of whom must be considered and whose pecuniary interests satisfied, diluting the funds, focus and energy that otherwise would be dedicated to the community and its residents.

Longshore Lake is in many ways unique in the Naples area. While more than 30 years old and competing with many newer communities, we have enduring features and amenities that they lack but are now in peril.
