OUR CONCERNS

CONCERN No. 1

Lack of Financial Reporting and Transparency

The current Board of Directors ran on a platform of fiduciary responsibility and oversight in addition to transparency. There has been no complete financial reporting (on the operating and DMCF budgets) to the residents and the Board's own Finance Advisory Committee since March. We would like to see a return to professionally prepared monthly financials which are required to effectively operate the Foundation. Now you know.

CONCERN No. 2

Return to in-house Management

After assuring the residents of Longshore Lake at Candidates Night, through emails published to the community, at the first Board of Directors Meeting in April through a PowerPoint presentation, and at a Coffee with the General Manager that the Board was committed to our own in-house management and staff employed by the Foundation, they quickly broke their word. At a meeting of the Past Presidents Council Mr. Marino assured the group that they would never hire a management company without a vote of the community. We need answers to the total costs and scope of the new contract. They assumed office in March and contacted KW Pr operty Management Company in April. No efforts were made to interview and hire employees as they resigned, except for the GM position. Now you know.

CONCERN No. 3

Lack of communication

To a group whose cornerstone of their campaign was “TRANSPARENCY”, we have seen little of it. The BOD has the bully pulpit of the email system and should be communicating with the residents on a weekly basis. When there isn’t clear directives and information…people just fill in the blanks with rumors and innuendo. Effective communication was promised. Now you know.

CONCERN No. 4

A Continuing Polarization of Longshore Lake

There has been and continues to be a schism in our wonderful community. The polarization of Longshore Lake residents is sad to observe. We know when emergencies strike- like hurricanes, all our neighbors come together to help one another. A priority for leadership should be the healing of this rift. We yearn for a return to a Longshore Lake where decisions made are member driven. “A house divided against itself can not stand”. Now you know.
